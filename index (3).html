Overview

Welcome to our exploration of the fascinating history behind the discovery of gravity. This fundamental force governs the motion of celestial bodies and shapes the universe as we know it.

Early Theories

Gravity has been a subject of contemplation for centuries. Ancient civilizations had various theories, but it wasn't until the 17th century that significant progress was made.

Galileo and Newton

Galileo Galilei made groundbreaking contributions to the understanding of gravity through his experiments with falling objects. However, it was Sir Isaac Newton who formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation in the late 17th century.

Modern Understanding

Since Newton's time, our comprehension of gravity has evolved with advancements in physics. Einstein's theory of general relativity revolutionized our understanding, explaining gravity as the curvature of spacetime.
